01

Always-on outbound engine

ClayLemlistSlackCustom ToolLinkedIn
Daily leadsNew conversations every day, on autopilot

The problem

Right-fit companies were visiting the site and leaving anonymously. There was no way to spot them, find the right people and reach out before the intent went cold.

What I built

An always-on system wiring together Clay, Lemlist, Slack and a custom tool. It tracks which companies visit the site, pulls the domain, identifies the right contacts by job title, and pushes them into parallel LinkedIn + email sequences across multiple senders — a connection request, a short LinkedIn follow-up and an email cadence running alongside.

The outcome

Because it runs continuously, the campaign generates fresh leads and new conversations every day — a steady, predictable flow of replies and booked calls instead of one-off blasts. A human-in-the-loop gate keeps quality high: every contact is approved before it enters Lemlist, and a Slack channel surfaces each company and contact in real time.

02

Inbound routing engine

Revenue OpsHubSpotNodeSlackRouting
11minMedian response, from 4 hours

The problem

Leads arrived from everywhere — website inbound, paid search, LinkedIn ads — into different tools and inboxes. HubSpot's round-robin couldn't account for fit, source or rep capacity, so high-intent demos went cold before anyone replied.

What I built

A routing engine that unifies every lead source — website forms, paid, LinkedIn ads and more — into one flow. It scores each lead, round-robins across the sales team by territory and live capacity, enrolls them in the right drip sequence based on score, and hands the rep full context — source, enrichment and history — so they open every conversation already informed.

The outcome

Median first response dropped from 4 hours to 11 minutes, the speed-to-lead SLA held above 95%, and no lead slipped through the cracks regardless of where it came from.

03

Custom Clay agents

ClayAI AgentsPythonEnrichmentScoring
+38%SQL → opportunity conversion

The problem

Reps burned hours on unqualified accounts. CRM records were thin and stale, and off-the-shelf enrichment couldn't answer the nuanced, research-style questions that actually decide fit.

What I built

Custom Clay AI agents that research each account the way a human SDR would — reading sites, news and signals to pull firmographic, technographic and intent data, qualify against our ICP, and write a clean fit score back to the CRM automatically.

The outcome

60k accounts agent-enriched and continuously refreshed; SQL-to-opportunity conversion up 38%.

04

Event-driven campaigns

EventsWeb ScrapingOutreachCustom ToolMeetings
Pre-bookedExec calendars, filled before each event

The problem

Conferences are full of right-fit buyers, but exec time on the ground is scarce. Leadership showed up without booked meetings or context on who was even worth meeting.

What I built

A two-part engine. Before the event, I scraped the web for the attendee list, identified the right accounts and ran targeted campaigns to book meetings onto the execs' calendars. Then a custom tool briefs each exec on every lead they'll meet — who's confirmed for the event day, the time slot they picked, and full context per contact — with a check to mark whether each meeting actually happened.

The outcome

Execs walked in with pre-booked, well-briefed meetings instead of cold floor-walking. And because the tool tracked which meetings happened, post-event follow-up campaigns went out automatically to pitch — turning event conversations into pipeline.
