I didn't start my career as a GTM engineer. I started in growth marketing — generating pipeline through demand generation, paid acquisition, lifecycle marketing, content and marketing automation. My job was to drive growth, lift conversions and help revenue teams hit their targets.

But over time, I noticed a pattern. The biggest obstacles to growth weren't always campaign-related — more often, they were operational. Leads weren't routed correctly. CRM data was incomplete. Attribution was unreliable. Marketing and sales worked from different sources of truth. Teams spent hours on manual tasks that should have been automated.

The work that moved the numbers most was the work behind the campaigns.

I found myself increasingly drawn to solving those problems. What began as building HubSpot workflows and improving marketing automation evolved into designing lead-routing systems, enrichment pipelines, attribution frameworks, outbound infrastructure and AI-powered GTM workflows.

The more I worked on the systems behind growth, the clearer it became: this was where I could create the greatest impact.

Today, as a GTM engineer, I combine a growth marketer's understanding of pipeline generation with the technical skills to build the infrastructure that powers it. My background means I don't just think about how systems work — I think about how they influence demand generation, conversion rates, sales productivity and revenue outcomes.

That mix of commercial thinking and technical execution lets me bridge the gap between strategy and systems — helping revenue teams scale more efficiently and turn data into predictable pipeline.
